The Vam Cong Cable-Stayed Bridge utilizes large steel beams from a Vietnamese enterprise
13rdAug2023
For the first time, a large-scale bridge project in Vietnam - the 2.97km Vam Cong Cable-Stayed Bridge - involves the participation of a Vietnamese enterprise, Thanh Long Corporation Joint Stock Company, in supplying large steel beams
9th of January in Ho Chi Minh city

On January 9th, in Ho Chi Minh City, Thanh Long Corporation Joint Stock Company, in collaboration with the consortium of contractors GS and Hanshin (South Korea), signed a cooperation agreement to provide technological support and consulting for the production and supply of steel structures for the Vam Cong Cable-Stayed Bridge project.

This is the first time a Vietnamese enterprise has participated in supplying large steel beams for a major bridge project in Vietnam.

VAM CONG BRIDGE
The 5.7 trillion bridge features the tallest towers and deepest drilled piles in Vietnam

According to the signed contract, the consortium of contractors GS and Hanshin will provide technological support, engineers, and consulting experts to assist Thanh Long Corporation Joint Stock Company in the production and supply of large steel beams for the Vam Cong Cable-Stayed Bridge project.

Mr. Vũ Thành Long, the CEO of Thanh Long Corporation, stated that the previous largest steel beam bridge projects in Vietnam, such as the Nhat Tan Bridge (Hanoi), the Dragon Bridge (Da Nang), and the Can Tho Bridge, were all supplied by major corporations from China and Japan. Therefore, the company's ability to secure a contract for the production and supply of steel structures for this project is crucial and demonstrates the credibility of the domestic steel industry.

The Vam Cong Bridge spans the Hau River, connecting Dong Thap province with Can Tho city, and is one of the four largest and most modern bridges in Vietnam.

It is an important transportation project aimed at gradually connecting the western region with the central area of the Mekong Delta, forming a second north-south corridor to alleviate the traffic load on National Highway 1A.

The project has a length of 2.97km and a total investment of over 271 million USD (approximately 5,687 billion VND) from preferential ODA funding from South Korea and the corresponding capital from Vietnam.

The steel structure section is the main bridge section with complex structures and large steel dimensions, consisting of 146 vertical beams and 225 horizontal beams, with a total steel structure mass of approximately 7,000 tons.

According to Mr. Vũ Thành Long, during the collaboration, Vietnamese engineers and workers will have the opportunity to access and learn from the experiences of Korean experts in this field. This is an important foundation for the development of the Vietnamese steel manufacturing industry.
